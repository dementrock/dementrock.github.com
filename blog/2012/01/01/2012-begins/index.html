2012 Begins

Jan 1st, 2012

I haven’t written such a thing as an annual summary for a very long time. It’s not that I’ve been busy, but I’m simply too lazy to write down these words. Instead I’ve too often kept these thoughts inside my head only.

Last year at the same time, I have just submitted my college applications. Then a few days later I began working on an MIT InvenTeam project in my high school. Interestingly, I have not joined the team until I happened to participate one of their team discussions and confidently promised that I had the skills to complete the project, which requires intensive web programming. But back to then, I knew nothing about web development at all. I was so lucky to have participated in this project because I received guidance from Tencent developers. I also learned a lot from a friend two grades lower than me. Without their help I might have spent much more time just to figure out what was actually going on.

Looking back, I have learned a lot about web during the year. Back to then, all I could include in my “resume” would be as following:

  • Experience in C, C++, Pascal
  • Done over 400 problems on various online judges.

Today the list of programming languages and techniques I’ve had experience is extended to Python, Ruby, Django, ROR, Java, Common Lisp, Javascript, Coffeescript, HTML, HAML, CSS, Sass, …

Comparing to what my peers have done, my experience is still far from enough. But I am still surprised by the progress I’ve made over the year.

What would I be if I did not happen to attend a team discussion and start the project? What would I be if I chose the common path as my other classmates and stay in China?

Life taught me not to ask these questions, since the progress of life itself is so delicate, so subtle, that I dare not to predict the outcome even with the slightest difference.

I cannot keep writing this way, or otherwise this post may never end. Let me just include some piece of thoughts this year:

  • College does not matter that much. I was once frustrated for not getting into schools such as MIT, Stanford or CalTech. But what makes the difference is really YOURSELF.
  • Well, for the 4 months in Berkeley, I’ve experienced so many things: earthquate, fire in the same building I was living, streaking…
  • It feels really great when I ranked 1st in an Abstract Algebra midterm.
  • Playing piano is a great way to get rid of troubles.
  • The most valuable thing I’ve bought this year is not my Macbook Air, but the Virtual Private Server which costs me $3/month. I learned so much from it.
  • I cofounded an Acapella. It’s still being incubated, but I hope to give some performance in the new year.
  • I ended the relationship I’ve been in for two years. Well, some things only time may do.
  • I was once so determined to set Artificial Intelligence as my dream. But after the year, I am not sure any more because of my rising interest in so many fields. But still the greatest accomplishment I’ve tasted still comes from solving a hard algorithm or math problem. Taking a course in AI soon may be the best way to make the decision.

Well, I’ve written a lot of nonsense. But the intention behind writing an annual summary is usually to set a goal for the new year. So here comes some plans:

  • I REALLY need to do some sport. For the whole year I have spent most of my time indoors. For the new year, the plan is to run 20 miles per week.

  • Make more friends. Well I admit I’m antisocial…

  • Read 25 books.

  • I’m not sure yet how to set a goal for piano.

  • Start taking grad courses in math, statistics, and computer science.
